## Vision

Most task managers are very good at one thing: collecting more tasks. Wisdone is built around the harder question — which of these actually matters. It connects goals to the work that advances them and surfaces the next right thing, so the list shrinks toward what counts instead of swelling toward what merely accumulates.

## The Problem

A backlog grows without bound because capture is easy and judgement is hard. The result is a tool that measures how much you could do, never how much of it mattered. Doing more of the wrong things feels like progress right up until nothing important has moved.

## Key Differentiators

- **Impact over volume**: the unit of value is finishing what matters, not logging what could be done.
- **Goal-linked tasks**: every item traces back to an outcome it advances, or it does not earn a place.
- **AI as a focusing lens**: the assistant points at the next right thing instead of summarizing the pile.
- **Capture without overhead**: getting work in is frictionless without turning the tool into another chore.
